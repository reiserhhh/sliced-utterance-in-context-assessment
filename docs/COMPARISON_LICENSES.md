# SUICA Comparison Licenses v1 — how far each score may be compared

Created: 2026-07-07, in answer to the user's concern after PRED-4: "if tiny
details can be determinants, cross-document comparison becomes impossible,
and even within-dataset cross-theme comparison feels unreliable."

Position: the concern is half right and half overcorrection. What the
evidence killed is UNIVERSAL comparability (levels and covariance geometry
across regimes, and any covariate shortcut). What the evidence KEPT is a
measured, graded ladder of valid comparisons. SUICA's answer to context
dependence is not despair but a LICENSE SYSTEM: every score carries an
explicit, evidence-priced statement of how far its comparisons reach.
(No questionnaire states this; they implicitly claim the widest license
without ever testing it — text made the rind VISIBLE, which is an
epistemic advantage dressed as bad news.)

## The transport ladder (what survives crossing contexts, measured)

| What you compare | Survives? | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Direction of construct-anchor relations | YES, strongest | H2 first_person->N: Reddit T4 +0.111, Essays dev +0.122 — two document types, two populations, same direction |
| Rank-order signal (correlations, prediction) | PARTIALLY (attenuated, nonzero) | PANDORA-fitted battery transported UNCHANGED to Essays: mean Big5 r 0.144 (~ bge transfer 0.153); F-family(4) alone carries N .150/C .119/A .117 |
| Within-platform cross-REGISTER | YES with per-register norms | OP-7a 19/19 robust (volume-matched + strict-separation sensitivity); level shifts only wcl_36/wcl_02 |
| Within-platform cross-THEME (venue) | YES for F-family, measured | class-disjoint retest IS the cross-theme reliability: wcl_60 0.365, first_person 0.268, wcl_13/23 0.169 (attenuation-uncorrected = conservative) |
| Score LEVELS across regimes | NO | register/production constraints shift levels (JP X sparsity; OP-7a dz; pro-drop) -> regime-relative norms mandatory |
| Covariance structure across regimes | NO | PRED-3 0.53-0.62; PRED-4 FAIL (no FORM shortcut) |
| Any of it via covariate adjustment | NO (theorem) | F4: self-selected type = mediator; adjustment deletes person signal |

## License grades (per construct family; earned, not assumed)

- **L0 — within person, within venue, across time** (monitoring/state use):
  all constructs incl. state ingredients; contiguous splits only (OP-15).
- **L1 — across themes/venues within platform**: F-family licensed at their
  class-disjoint coefficients (0.17-0.37 uncorrected); composites licensed
  WITH two-channel annotation; C-family NOT licensed as style comparisons —
  category error; their cross-theme content IS the choice channel, compare
  them as choice profiles instead.
- **L2 — across registers within platform**: all 19 licensed (OP-7a),
  per-register norms required for wcl_36/wcl_02 (and any future dz>=0.5).
- **L3 — across platforms/document types**: direction and rank-order ONLY;
  levels forbidden; regime-relative norms; anchor recalibration expected;
  definitive person-level comparability requires within-person multi-regime
  data (OP-35 — no statistical shortcut exists, PRED-4).
- **L4 — across languages**: nothing licensed until within-language
  re-validation (D1; JP protocol stage 3).

The residual/identity layer (OP-32) is license-EXEMPT in one sense:
identification and distinctive-profile claims are within-population
statements and never require cross-context level comparability.

## The two kinds of "tiny determinants" (they need opposite responses)

1. **Pipeline minutiae** (smart apostrophes, tokenizer order, tie handling,
   rng streams, refit-vs-transport): these are DETERMINISM issues, and they
   are CONTROLLED — measured impacts: OP-18a max headline delta 0.002;
   tokenizer-order divergence 2/135,115 slices; and the audit chain caught
   every violation (9 builder errors in 10 rounds). Frozen conventions +
   equivalence gates + wrong-world licenses are the standing answer.
2. **Situational minutiae** (venue, audience, occasion, spontaneity): these
   are NOT noise and NOT nuisance — they are the rind, and the theory's
   central result is that they CARRY signal (choice channel) or MODULATE
   it (register). The response is channel accounting (per-construct flesh/
   choice shares) and this license table, not statistical suppression.

## Governance rule (G12, to be added to the AI-analyst guide)

Every reported score comparison must name its license grade (L0-L4). A
comparison outside the license (e.g., level comparison of tension between
a Reddit user and an essay writer) must be refused by the analyst with a
pointer to this document. Forbidden phrase without a license: "higher/
lower than" across regimes; permitted: "in the same direction as",
"ranked similarly within their own populations".

## Honest statement of the current limits

Within-platform, SUICA comparisons are in reliability territory normal for
short psychological instruments (cross-theme flesh 0.17-0.37 uncorrected;
within-register up to SB 0.86 at volume). Across platforms, SUICA today
supports directional and rank-order claims only — which is exactly what it
proved at T4. Level-comparable cross-regime measurement is not impossible
in principle; it requires the within-person multi-regime design (OP-35),
i.e., it is a DATA-COLLECTION problem, not a broken-theory problem.
