# Theory-Paper Style Notes v1 — what the exemplars teach

Created: 2026-07-07, per the user's redirection: the paper should LOGICALLY
PROVE the theory, with the development/validation story compressed to
support. Exemplars studied and the argumentation devices to borrow.

## Exemplars and their devices

**Mischel & Shoda (1995, Psych Review) — the archetype.**
- Device 1: OPEN WITH A PARADOX two literatures cannot both survive
  (personality invariance vs. cross-situational variability), then DISSOLVE
  it rather than pick a side: both camps saw part of one system.
- Device 2: **make the error term the phenomenon** — situational
  variability, treated for decades as noise, IS the personality signature
  (if A then X, if B then Y).
- Direct lineage for us: SUICA repeats the move one level down — the
  variance that language-based assessment deletes as "topic nuisance" IS
  the choice channel; and our C3 is literally their behavioral signature,
  in text. The paper should claim this ancestry explicitly.

**Borsboom, Mellenbergh & van Heerden (2004, Psych Review).**
- Device 3: one crisp ontological definition does the work of pages ("a
  test is valid iff the attribute exists and causally produces the score
  variations"). State the theory as few, sharp, necessary-and-sufficient
  claims; derive; stop.
- Device 4: name the rival tradition's hidden commitment (epistemology/
  correlation) and replace it (ontology/causality). Ours: the hidden
  commitment is CONTEXT EXOGENEITY, silently imported from experimental
  design into observational corpora; we replace it with context-as-chosen-
  behavior.

**Buss (1987, JPSP) — selection/evocation/manipulation.**
- Device 5: mechanisms of person-environment correspondence give the
  theory ancestors. Our C1 = SELECTION formalized for verbal ecologies;
  venue-evoked patterning (C3) inherits evocation.

**Rauthmann et al. (2015-, EJP) — situation research principles.**
- Situation taxonomies and trait-congruent situation experience supply the
  modern empirical base for axiom A2 (choice distributions are
  person-stable and trait-congruent). Our venue classes are an empirical
  situation taxonomy for text.

**Brunswik — representative design.**
- Device 6: assessment must sample the organism's ECOLOGY representatively;
  systematic (fixed-item) designs buy control at the cost of
  generalization. Slicing a person's accumulated writing IS representative
  design for verbal behavior; the questionnaire is the systematic design.
  This turns our "free collection + design-based control" from preference
  into methodological principle with a classical pedigree.

**Fleeson & Jayawickreme (2015, JRP) — Whole Trait Theory.**
- Device 7: enumerate the theory as N numbered commitments (their five
  points); descriptive side (density distributions of states) separated
  from explanatory side. Our slices-per-person are literally a density
  distribution of verbal states; trait scores are its parameters.

**Meehl (1978) — risky predictions.**
- Device 8: a theory earns credit by what it FORBIDS. Dedicate a section to
  prohibitions (centering under self-selection; cross-regime level
  comparison; stability-as-trait without purity) and to standing risky
  predictions (assigned-context boundary; form-concentration; prospective
  seals). Report our own failed omnibus as the discipline working.

## Structural template adopted (v2 manuscript)

1. The paradox (predictive success built on topical features vs. practice
   that deletes topical variance; stability readable without any style).
2. Theoretical background — four pillars (situation selection; behavioral
   signatures; representative design; stylometry/idiolect + density
   distributions; idiographic residual as fifth).
3. The theory — three axioms, three channels as consequences, the formal
   core (composite mean; centering-as-mediation theorem with boundary
   condition; purity identification; license corollaries).
4. What the theory explains (the sweep over established findings).
5. What the theory forbids and predicts (risky content; empirical status
   TABLE compresses the entire validation program to one exhibit).
6. The idiographic completion (position/configuration/residual).
7. Implications for assessment practice + one-paragraph epistemic
   discipline (freeze/seal/license/audit), supplements carry the machinery.

## Prose rules

- Dissolve, don't win: both existing camps kept partial truths.
- Ontological verbs: choice IS behavior; contexts ARE selected; deletion,
  not cleaning.
- One formal statement per idea, in words first, symbols second.
- Numbers appear as (a) decisive falsifiers, (b) boundary demonstrations,
  (c) one status table — never as a validation travelogue.
- First person plural, present tense for the theory, past for evidence.
