# SUICA V6 Lineage Compatibility Synthesis

## Decision

V6 does **not** invalidate the successful V3/V4 fixed-rind result. The
versions estimate different objects and should remain in the same framework:

\[
\begin{aligned}
Q_u^{\mathrm{fixed}} &= \mathcal L(X_{ui}\mid A_{ui}=a_u),\\
Q_u^{\mathrm{natural}} &= \mathcal L(A_{ui},X_{ui},\Delta t_{ui}),\\
Q_u^{\mathrm{resid}} &= \mathcal L(X_{ui}-\widehat E_{D}[X\mid A_i]).
\end{aligned}
\]

`Q_fixed` is a conditional-expression precision object, `Q_natural` is the
author-selected natural process, and `Q_resid` is a reference-relative
deviation geometry. None is a personality construct without separate
psychological validation.

## What remains true from earlier versions

1. Fixing the rind by design can increase the precision of specific
   token-slice expression coordinates inside a population. This is a design
   principle, not an instruction to subtract every contextual difference.
2. Natural choice of topic/community remains part of a person's text process.
   J1 deliberately retains it rather than treating it as removable noise.
3. A population condition subtraction changes the geometry. It can sharpen a
   multivariate author-matching task while making individual coordinate scores
   less repeatable. It therefore cannot replace raw or fixed-rind scores by
   default.
4. The older `tension` claim is unchanged: its historical positive effect was
   previously traced to temporal clustering. Its positive token-slice result
   here is descriptive and does not promote it to a stable trait coordinate.

## Coverage of the version-to-version check

| Historical property | Earlier status | Current V6 treatment |
|---|---|---|
| PRED-1 fixed-rind precision | Audited support for first-person, directive, novelty | Directly rechecked. Upheld at the original token-slice unit; event-disjoint counterpart is support-limited, not negative. |

| PRED-2 domain-fixed micro-choice on X | Pass with a small-sample caveat | Not rerun here: it is an X-specific selection claim, not a PANDORA V6 replacement test. Its old caveat remains binding. |
| PRED-3 cross-corpus covariance transport | Partial only | Not treated as a strong legacy theorem. V6 does not inherit a universal cross-rind coefficient claim. |

This audit is therefore a targeted compatibility closure, not a blanket
re-execution of every historical exploratory script.

## Consequence for V6

The correct architecture is not “V6 replaces all earlier methods.” It is a
typed measurement system:

| Use case | Licensed object | Current boundary |
|---|---|---|
| Fixed prompt/rind assessment | `Q_fixed` | Token-slice conditional-expression precision; needs a specified prompt/rind and sufficient text. |
| Natural posts / free text | `Q_natural` | Selection-plus-expression geometry; not personality or causal separation. |
| Exploratory condition comparison | `Q_resid` | Sensitivity geometry only; report alongside raw coordinates, never as an automatically cleaner score. |
| Motion/order | No promoted human endpoint | J3 is refused; use only synthetic license until a richer repeated-condition corpus exists. |

The next theoretical work should keep these objects separate and ask only
questions whose support matches the desired unit: token-slice reliability,
event-process geometry, or genuinely repeated same-person response dynamics.
